ABOUT OUR EXCHANGE

From 20th to 27th may 2023 our German friends were staying in the school's dormitory as a part of our student exchange. Our friends from Wuppertal came to Poland to learn about our country and it's history. The whole enterprise is organized as a part of project "Zachować pamięć". As students of second class in ZSTI gliwice we have helped and accompanied them with pleasure during this exchange. This international project allowed us to improve our German speaking skills, make a new friends and get to know other pepole's opinion about our country. Such international cooperation taught us tolerance and respect for the history of our countries.

Welcome
Willa Caro, outdoor game - Day 1

The first day was based on getting to know the girls who visited us. We all met together in front of our school's dormitory building, none of us knew what to expect. The plan for saturday was to visit Villa Caro and take a part in the field game that our teachers had prepared. When everyone arrived, our teachers decided that we would take our first photo together. Later we had to split into groups, each was supposed to consist of 3 boys and 3 girls. Once we were all matched up, the time to get to know with the girls began. We quietly made our way to our final destination, which was the museum. Many of us had not visited this place before, we were surprised by the nature of the place. We watched the local specific paintings and other exhibits that were presented there. It was time for a field game, together in groups we followed tasks our teachers had designed. We had finished on the market square at the Neptune fountain, then we headed to the school to see off our new friends , in anticipation of the next day's sightseeing

Wroclaw - Day 2

That day as any other started with our meeting with German group. Our trip was planned at 7.45 and so we left then. In bus we get to know each others musical taste. That's how we spent hours, till we finally arrived our destiantion. Our trip started from sightseeing Wroclaw's Sky Tower and gallery. Some of us before heading to one of the highest sightseeing point in Poland have choosed to eat something. Nourished by well-balanced meal we headed to the Tower. The view was incredible. Almost all the city was in the range of our sight. Unfortunately this experience wasn't lasting long and we had to go down by the elevator. After quick break and exchanging our impressions, we went on sightseeing the city.





We were devided into polish group and german group, each of groups got a guide. Trip was very interesting, and we were told a lot of interesting historic facts about the city. At the end of our journey was a marketplace, where we could freely move around and sightsee. After longer period of time we got together and went to our last stop during trip and it was the "Hall of the century". On the way, we saw a bus with Legia Warsaw's basketball players, they were very contented with cheering which we gave them. At the place as like as on the marketplace we got free hand about moving around and we had an occasion to go sightsee that place. It wasn't lasting too long since our teachers told us, that we had to go back.

Auschwitz I - Day 3

It is the third day with our german friends . Today we went with them to a place known for a lot of crimes, commited by Germans on Jews. We were divided into two groups: German and Polish. It took us four hours to explore the entire camp. We were all traumatized, when we saw all those extermination sites and what unacceptable things can be done to a human. Our moods were improved by a delicious dinner. Nourished and ready to go we went to the last thing that awaited us, which was a workshop entilted “The Eleventh. dont be indifferent”. The lady talked about the fact, that Auschwitz did not happen suddenly, but was made by a sequence of events, that added to it and no one responded to them. She also showed us how easily a stereotype turns into a prejudice, and next into discrimination. It also showed how people discriminate against each other through the prism of one thing. The workshops did not last long and we had to go home.


Auschwitz II - Day 4


The next day, next adventure. In today's plan was guide tour around second concentration camp located in Brzezinka. It was an experience that exhausted us physically and mentally. The conditions in which the prisoners lived in this camp were unhuman. The suffering they felt is unimaginable to human mind. After a long time, our journey through the camp has come to an end. Our plan's considered a trip to Katowice, but the fatigue and sadness coming from the camp have overcame our German friends.

Kraków - Day 5

One before last day of our student exchange. Our today's target was to visit a Krakow, specifially a tour of Kazimierz, the district where the Jews live. We were split to two groups devided by country. Every group had their own guide. During this time we visited many interesting places especially the synagogues. After the introductory tour of Kazimierz, the time came to go eat something. Most of us went to try the famous casseroles in Krakow. They were delicious. Our last attraction was a Jewisch Museum ,,Galicja", where we had workshops ,,From stereotype to discrimination", which where very similar to these in Oświęcim. After the workshops we had a meeting with Lida Maksymowicz, that have survived the Auschwitz-Birkenau and she told us her story from her life. The story of Lidia Maksymowicz was very tragic and exciting in the same time, which moved most of us to tears. At the end of this day we come back to our lovely city like everyday.

Radiostation, working on project, farewell party in Park Slaski - Day 6

On Thursday we met adt 8:20, as usual in front of the boarding school. When everyone had arrived, we set off towards the Gliwice Radiostation. We arrived around 9:00. We splited into 2 groups (Polish and German). We watched film about German propaganda from 1939, related to the history of the radiostation, and we had conversation with a guide who presented us with the whole fate of facility. We stayed in rooms in authentic furnishings and equipment from the early 20th century. At the end of the trip, we took a photo together. The time we had arrived to school, we started working on the film and on the webside.
