Powitanie - Palmiarnia, gra terenowa - Dzień 1

Palmiarnia została zniszczona w 1945 roku. Odbudowano ją praktycznie od podstaw. W 1998 roku oddano do użytku nowoczesne obiekty szklarniowe. Obecnie powierzchnia Palmiarni liczy 2000 metrów kwadratowych; najwyższy budynek ma 22 m. W czterech pawilonach tematycznych możemy podziwiać egzotyczne rośliny użytkowe, rośliny tropikalne, sukulenty, a w pawilonie historycznym – palmy, a wśród nich 120-letnie feniksy kanaryjskie. W ostatnich latach powstały cztery ogromne akwaria, w których odtworzone zostały ekosystemy Amazonki, jeziora Tanganika, rzeki azjatyckiej i polskiej.

Wrocław - Dzień 2

Hala Stulecia to zabytkowa hala widowiskowo-sportowa, która została wybudowana na wystawę w 1913 roku. Znajduje się ona na terenie Wrocławskiego Centrum Kongresowego i jest jednym z najważniejszych zabytków miasta. Wewnątrz hali znajduje się m.in. basen olimpijski oraz scena widowiskowa, na której odbywają się liczne koncerty i wydarzenia kulturalne.





Festung Breslau is the name of the fortress and fortification system that were built in Wrocław during World War II by German troops. The purpose of these fortifications was to defend the city against the atacks of the Soviet army.

The fortress consisted of several lines of defense, including a system of fortifications built around the Old Town and many bunkers and shelters. Many residents of Wrocław were forced to work on the construction of Festung Breslau, and the fortification system itself became one of the last bastions of German defense during the battle for the city.

After the capitulation of the fortress on May 6, 1945, Festung Breslau was mostly destroyed, and many of the fortifications remained only as ruins. Today, the remaining parts of the fortress are open to the public and they remind us of the dramatic events during the war.



Auschwitz I - Dzień 3

Auschwitz Birkenau is a German concentration and extermination camp that was created during World War II in the town called Brzezinka near Oświęcim. The camp was part of the German policy of extermination of nations and became a symbol of German Nazi terror and one of the biggest genocide places places in history.

In Auschwitz Birkenau, the Germans murdered about 1.1 million people, mainly Jews, but also Poles, Romani people and people with other nationalities. In the camp, medical experiments were carried out on prisoners, and they were also used as labor for forced labour.

The camp consisted of three parts: Auschwitz I, Auschwitz II-Birkenau and Auschwitz III-Monowitz, where labor camps were located. In Auschwitz I there were camp administration offices, magazines and prisoner rooms, where mainly political prisoners were kept, and in Birkenau there were gas chambers and crematories, where mass murders were made.

Today, Auschwitz Birkenau is a museum and memorial place that reminds the tragic history of the camp and its victims. In the museum, you can see the remains of barracks, gas chambers and crematories, as well as many personal items such as documents and luggage that the prisoners left behind. The museum is visited by crowds of tourists and it is one of the most important places of memory and reflection in the world.


Auschwitz II, Katowice - Dzień 4


Obóz Auschwitz II łączył w sobie dwie funkcje:
• ośrodka bezpośredniej zagłady Żydów. W Birkenau powstały największe w okupowanej Europie urządzenia masowej zagłady – komory gazowe – gdzie niemieccy naziści zamordowali większość deportowanych do obozu Żydów. Gromadzono w nim również i systematycznie zabijano chorych i wyselekcjonowanych na śmierć z całego kompleksu obozów;
• kompleksu koncentracyjnego, składającego się z obozów o różnym charakterze i przeznaczeniu (m.in. tzw. obóz cygański, tzw. obóz rodzinny dla Żydów z getta Theresienstadt, magazyny zagrabionego mienia). W ostatniej fazie istnienia stał się też miejscem koncentracji i punktem dystrybucji siły roboczej dla niemieckiego przemysłu w głębi Trzeciej Rzeszy. Zadaniem obozu Auschwitz III był przede wszystkim wynajem niewolniczej siły roboczej więźniów firmom niemieckim, dlatego skupił on podobozy zakładane przy pobliskich przedsiębiorstwach przemysłowych.

The Silesian Museum in Katowice is one of the largest and most important museums in Poland, dedicated to history and culture of Silesia region Museum was created in 1929 and since then it became an important culture and science center.

In the museum you can see many valuable monuments related to the history and culture of Silesia, including work of art, everyday objects, documents and pictures. Among the most important exhibits are: archaeological collections related to prehistoric cultures, a collections of work of art, as well as rich collections on the history of industry, mining and metallurgy in Silesia.

Museum is located in a historic building that was once the seat of a German court. In 2015, a new wing of the museum was opened, which was designed with the use of the latest technologies, which enables an interactive and attractive form of presenting the exhibits.

Silesian Museum in Katowice is important place for residents of this region as well as tourists that want to get to know Silesian history and culture.


Kraków - Dzień 5

Kraków is beautiful city in Poland with great history and cultural heritage. In Kraków there are many important places connected to history of Jews and Holocaust.

Galicia Jewish Museum is a museum dedicated to the culture and history of Galician Jews. There are exhibitions of pictures, documents and artifacts that show the life and traditions of Jews in Galicia before World War II.

Kazimierz is the former Jewish district of Kraków, which is full of monuments and culture. You can see many synagogues, Jewish cemeteries, as well as restaurants and shops that offer Jewish cuisine and souvenirs there.

The Holocaust is one of the greatest genocides in history that took place during World War II and cost the lives of millions of people, most of them were Jewish. There are many memorial places in Kraków, including the Oskar Schindler Museum, which presents the story of rescuing the Jews by a German industrialist, and the Emalia Factory, which was one of the workplaces for prisoners of the concentration camp in Płaszów.

Kraków is important places of memory and reflection, as well as full of beautiful monuments and culture.
