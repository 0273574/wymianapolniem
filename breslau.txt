            <p>Festung Breslau is the name of the fortress and fortification system that were built in Wrocław during
                World War II by German troops. The purpose of these fortifications was to defend the city against the atacks of the Soviet army.
                <br><br>
                The fortress consisted of several lines of defense, including a system of fortifications built around the Old Town and many bunkers and shelters.
                Many residents of Wrocław were forced to work on the construction of Festung Breslau, 
                and the fortification system itself became one of the last bastions of German defense during the battle for the city.
                <br><br>
                After the capitulation of the fortress on May 6, 1945, Festung Breslau was mostly destroyed, and many of the fortifications remained only as ruins.
                Today, the remaining parts of the fortress are open to the public and they remind us of the dramatic events during the war.
            </p><br><img src="images/festung.jpg" width="500px" height="300px"><br>
